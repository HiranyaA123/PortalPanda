Morning

Hoppers, fermented overnight

The batter goes down the evening before — rice flour, coconut milk, a whisper of yeast — and by opening it's alive. Each one is swirled into a screaming-hot bowl-pan so the edges go lacy and brittle while the centre stays soft as custard. Crack an egg in, spoon lunu miris on the side, drink it with plain tea.

Midday

The rice & curry table

Trays go into the bain-marie at eleven and come out when they're gone — no reheats, no tomorrow. You point, we plate: rice first, then your protein, then three vegetables, then a spoon of gravy over everything and a papadam on top. It's the way lunch has been eaten on the island for a very long time.

Kottu on the flat-top

You'll hear it from the carpark — two blades against steel, chopping roti through curry, egg and leek in a rhythm every Sri Lankan knows by heart. It's loud, it's late, it's the best thing to eat after a long day. Cheese kottu if you're feeling reckless.

Why "Colompoor"

It's how home sounds when you say it fast.

Everything here is made the slow way — coconut milk squeezed each morning, spices roasted and ground in-house, sambols pounded rather than blitzed. Nothing arrives in a jar.
